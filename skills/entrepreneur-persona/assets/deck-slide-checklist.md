# Pitch deck slide checklist

## Visual design rules (every slide)

- [ ] **One message per slide** — if you need two points, use two slides
- [ ] **30pt+ font minimum** — if judges can't read it from the back row, it's too small
- [ ] **Max 6 lines of text** — fewer is better; aim for 3–4
- [ ] **Billboard test** — can someone understand the slide in 3 seconds from 10 feet away?
- [ ] **High contrast** — dark text on light background or vice versa; no light gray on white
- [ ] **2 colors maximum** (plus black/white) — brand consistency, not a rainbow
- [ ] **Images over bullets** — one strong image communicates faster than five bullet points
- [ ] **No embedded videos** — they fail in competition settings; use screenshots or stills with captions
- [ ] **No clip art or stock photos** — use product photos, diagrams, or clean icons
- [ ] **Consistent alignment** — left-align text; center-align only for single-line titles
- [ ] **White space is your friend** — crowded slides signal unclear thinking

## Slide-specific visual guidance

| Slide | Visual approach |
|-------|-----------------|
| 1. Hook | Full-bleed image or single bold stat — no bullets |
| 2. Problem | Photo of the person experiencing the problem, or a stark data point |
| 3. Solution | Product screenshot, mockup, or demo still — show, don't describe |
| 4. Market | TAM/SAM/SOM funnel diagram with numbers — no paragraph text |
| 5. Why Now? | Timeline or trend chart showing the inflection point |
| 6. Competition | 2×2 matrix or positioning chart — avoid dense feature comparison tables |
| 7. Traction | Bar chart or timeline with numbers — no narrative paragraphs |
| 8. Business Model | Simple flow diagram: customer → product → revenue |
| 9. Go-to-Market | Phased roadmap visual (Phase 1 → 2 → 3) |
| 10. Use of Funds | Pie chart or horizontal bar with $ and % labels |
| 11. Team | Headshots + name + one-line credential (what you shipped) — no paragraphs |
| 12. Ask + Close | Single bold sentence on screen; you deliver the emotion verbally |

## Common mistakes

- Putting your company logo on every slide (wastes space; judges know who you are)
- Reading bullet points verbatim (slides support your talk, not replace it)
- Animating everything (transitions distract; judges watch YOU, not effects)
- Using a dark theme in a bright room (projectors wash out dark backgrounds)
- Cramming two ideas into one slide to "save time" (it costs clarity, which costs attention)

## Export tools

- **Gamma** (gamma.app) — AI-generated decks from markdown outlines; fastest path from content to designed slides
- **Google Slides / Keynote / PowerPoint** — paste the skill's markdown outline, one slide per section
- **Canva** — template-based approach; good for non-designers
- The skill produces **content and structure**; the user or a design tool handles visual execution
