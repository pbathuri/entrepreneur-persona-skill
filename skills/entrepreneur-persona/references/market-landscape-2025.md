# Market landscape & red flags (2025–2026)

## Purpose

Surface these considerations when evaluating ventures in the current environment.
This file should be refreshed annually. Current as of early 2026.

---

## VC funding environment

### Macro conditions
- **Post-ZIRP reality:** cost of capital matters; VCs demand capital efficiency over growth-at-all-costs
- Median seed round: $3–4M (2025); Series A bar: $1–2M ARR or equivalent traction proof
- Dry powder exists but deployment is selective; unit economics scrutiny is at all-time highs
- AI/ML companies attract disproportionate capital but face heightened scrutiny on defensibility
- Investor attention span: average 2 minutes 18 seconds per deck review (2025 data)

### What VCs look for (2025–2026)
- Path to profitability, not just growth rate
- **Capital efficiency:** revenue per dollar of funding raised
- Founder-market fit with demonstrated unfair advantage
- Clear moat beyond "we use AI" - data flywheel, network effects, regulatory positioning
- Evidence of **retention**, not just acquisition
- LTV:CAC ratio ≥ 3:1 with realistic cohort data

## Red flags that sink pitches

### AI-specific red flags
- **AI wrapper without moat:** Using OpenAI/Anthropic/Google API with a UI on top and no proprietary data, fine-tuning, or workflow integration. Ask: "What happens when the foundation model provider launches this feature natively?"
- **Inflated AI TAM:** Claiming TAM based on "all knowledge workers" or "every company that uses [X]." Demand beachhead specificity and bottom-up math.
- **No data strategy:** AI products without a plan for proprietary data accumulation or feedback loops that improve the product over time
- **Hallucination risk unaddressed:** Products where AI errors have real consequences (medical, legal, financial) without guardrails, human-in-the-loop, or confidence thresholds
- **Model dependency:** Single-model dependency (one API provider) without fallback, abstraction layer, or contingency if pricing doubles or API changes

### Regulatory red flags (current)
- **EU AI Act** (enforcing 2025–2026): High-risk AI classification requirements, mandatory transparency, prohibited use categories (social scoring, real-time biometric ID). Ask: "Have you assessed your AI Act risk classification?"
- **FDA SaMD** (Software as Medical Device): If software makes clinical decisions or informs treatment, it likely needs FDA clearance (510(k), De Novo, or PMA). Many founders underestimate timeline (6–18 months) and cost ($50K–$500K+).
- **State privacy laws:** CCPA/CPRA (CA), CTDPA (CT), VCDPA (VA), and 10+ state laws create a patchwork. Federal privacy law still absent but state compliance burden is real.
- **FTC enforcement:** Increased action on AI claims ("AI-powered" without substance), dark patterns, and data practices
- **Banking/fintech:** CFPB enforcement posture, state money transmitter licenses, evolving stablecoin and crypto regulation

### Business model red flags
- **Growth at all costs:** Negative unit economics with "we'll figure out monetization later" - the 2021 playbook is dead
- **Vanity metrics:** Downloads, page views, or "users" without activation, retention, or engagement definition
- **"No competitors":** Almost never true; signals lack of market research - judges and VCs will Google during your pitch
- **Precision without method:** "47.3% of millennials prefer..." without sample, method, date, or source
- **Hockey-stick without channel logic:** Revenue projections that don't connect to a realistic acquisition funnel with conversion rates at each stage

## Macro-to-micro economic integration

### Macro level (surface when relevant to the venture)
- **Interest rate environment:** Effect on consumer spending, business capex, and startup fundraising (higher rates = higher bar for returns)
- **Industry-specific cycles:** Real estate, retail, enterprise software renewal, seasonal demand patterns
- **Labor market conditions:** Effect on hiring plans, team costs, and competition for talent
- **Geopolitical factors:** Supply chain risks (semiconductor access, tariffs, trade restrictions, shipping disruptions)
- **Consumer confidence:** Effect on discretionary spending for B2C ventures

### Industry level (always surface)
- **Competitive consolidation:** Are incumbents acquiring or building? (M&A wave = both threat and exit opportunity)
- **Regulatory trajectory:** Is the regulatory environment tightening or loosening in this sector?
- **Technology maturation:** Is the enabling technology ready for production or still experimental?
- **Platform risk:** Dependency on Apple/Google/Amazon/Meta policies (App Store rules, API changes, algorithm shifts)

### Micro level (always surface)
- **Unit economics at current scale vs projected scale** - show the bridge, not just the destination
- **Customer concentration risk** - is >20% of revenue from one customer?
- **Working capital and cash conversion cycle** - how long from spend to revenue collection?
- **Burn rate and implied runway** - months of operation at current spend rate

## Timing framework: "Why Now?" analysis

For every venture, explicitly address all five dimensions:

1. **Technology enabler:** What technology matured or became affordable that makes this possible now but not 3 years ago? (e.g., LLM costs dropped 10x in 2 years; edge computing enables real-time IoT)
2. **Regulatory shift:** What law, rule, or enforcement change creates the opening? (e.g., EU AI Act creates compliance market; FDA De Novo pathway for novel devices)
3. **Behavioral change:** What consumer or business behavior shifted? (e.g., post-COVID remote work normalization; Gen Z digital-native purchasing; AI tool adoption curve)
4. **Market structure change:** What M&A, market exit, or incumbent failure created the gap? (e.g., competitor acquired and product sunset; industry consolidation leaving underserved segments)
5. **Economic/funding condition:** Why is the capital environment favorable for this specific approach now? (e.g., VCs actively seeking capital-efficient AI plays; government grants for climate tech via IRA)

**Threshold:** If a venture cannot answer at least 2 of 5 convincingly, the "Why Now?" is weak - flag it and prescribe research to strengthen the timing thesis.
