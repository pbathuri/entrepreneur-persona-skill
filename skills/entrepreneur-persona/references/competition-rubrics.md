# Competition rubrics (Clapp-style synthesis)

Use this to **predict judge scoring** and to **prioritize** what to fix before submission or finals.

## Three key evaluation factors

1. **Uniqueness** — Is the insight real? Is there a “secret” or non-obvious wedge beyond “we’re cheaper”?  
2. **Clarity** — Can a smart non-expert understand the business in ~5 minutes without jargon hand-waving?  
3. **Viability** — Is there a credible path to revenue, delivery, and follow-on milestones with honest risks named?

## Detailed rubric (score 1–5 per category)

| Category | What “5” looks like | What “1” looks like |
|----------|---------------------|---------------------|
| **Problem clarity & severity** | Specific who/when/cost; painkiller not vitamin | Vague “big problem”; no frequency or urgency |
| **Solution elegance & feasibility** | Clear mechanism; realistic v1; credible build plan | Feature soup; magic tech; no delivery path |
| **Market understanding & sizing** | TAM→SAM→SOM with sources; beachhead named | “Huge market” without logic or sourcing |
| **Competitive awareness** | Honest landscape; differentiation beyond price | “We have no competitors” |
| **Customer validation evidence** | Method + numbers + next test | Opinions or anecdotes only |
| **Business model & unit economics** | How you make money; gross margin story; assumptions stated | Buzzwords; no math; no CAC/LTV awareness when relevant |
| **Team & founder–problem fit** | Relevant skills; lived insight; advisors/partners named | Generic “passion” without capability map |
| **Presentation & time** | Story arc; respects limits; crisp visuals | Dense text; overtime; reading slides |
| **Q&A** | Direct answers; admits unknowns; depth on validation | Deflection; contradictions; hostility |
| **Investability / fundability** | Clear milestones; capital efficiency; sensible ask | Uncapped ambition; no use of funds logic |

## How to use with founders

- Pick the **lowest two scores** and assign **one concrete experiment** each.  
- Re-run after new evidence; don’t polish slides to hide thin validation.
