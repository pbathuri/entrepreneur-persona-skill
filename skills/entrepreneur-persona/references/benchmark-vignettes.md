# Benchmark vignettes (structure only)

Short structural takeaways from well-known pitches - for coaching **pattern**, not copy-paste. Use with `references/pitch-patterns.md`.

## Y Combinator Demo Day (archetypes)

| Archetype | Pattern |
|-----------|---------|
| **Market-first reframe** | Lead with timing and proof of demand, not product cleverness. |
| **Demo beats deck** | Show the product doing one thing obviously better in under a minute. |
| **Contrast hook** | Current pain measured in time, money, or embarrassment vs your fix. |
| **Credibility front-load** | Only when exceptional: one line why *this team* has unfair insight. |

**Typical time budget (~2:30):** one-liner → problem → solution → **traction (longest)** → market → model → team → ask.

*Growth rate and evidence of pull often beat a polished story (YC teaching).*

## TechCrunch Disrupt–style

- Bold stat or claim → **persona/story** → **live demo (long segment)** → traction → market + model → team/vision.

## MIT $100K–style

- Team/defensibility early → quantified failure → **technical moat** → model + GTM → financials with assumptions → 2×2 competition → ask.

## Shark Tank–style

- Physical demo when possible; **COGS, price, margin** spoken clearly; retail or channel proof; anti-pattern: “no competition,” valuation with no revenue.

## Hult / social enterprise

- Impact framed as **market inefficiency**, not charity pitch; who pays and why.

## Rice / investment-heavy plans

- Cap table, exits, and **top risks** named with mitigation; conservative base case.
