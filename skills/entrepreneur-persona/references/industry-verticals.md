# Industry verticals - supplementary guidance

## How to use this file

When a user describes their venture, identify the primary vertical from the list below.
Surface the relevant section's considerations during WF1 (validation), WF2 (proposal Section 4: Operations),
WF3 (deck slide structure), and WF5 (gauntlet questions). Do not dump all verticals - only activate
the matching one.

## Vertical detection prompt

After the user states their idea, classify into one of: SaaS, Marketplace, Hardware/IoT, Biotech/Healthtech,
CPG/D2C, Fintech, Edtech, Climate/Energy, or General. If unclear, ask:
"Is your product primarily software-as-a-service, a marketplace connecting two sides, a physical product,
health/biotech, consumer packaged goods, or financial services?"

## SaaS

### Key metrics to surface
- MRR/ARR, churn rate (monthly/annual), NDR (net dollar retention), expansion revenue, cohort analysis
- CAC payback period, LTV:CAC ratio (target >3:1)
- Free-to-paid conversion rate, trial-to-close cycle length

### Industry-specific validation questions
- What is your monthly churn rate, and how does it compare to SaaS benchmarks (<5% monthly for SMB, <1% for enterprise)?
- What does your NDR look like - are customers expanding or contracting?
- How long is your sales cycle, and how does it affect cash flow?
- What is your magic number (net new ARR / sales & marketing spend)?

### Proposal/deck adjustments
- Section 5 (Financial): Include MRR waterfall (new + expansion − contraction − churn)
- Slide 7 (Business Model): Show cohort retention curve if available
- Section 4 (Operations): Infrastructure costs, uptime SLAs, data security posture

## Marketplace

### Key metrics
- GMV vs net revenue (take rate), liquidity (matches per time unit), supply/demand ratio
- Chicken-and-egg: which side do you subsidize first?
- Leakage rate (transactions moving off-platform), disintermediation risk
- Repeat rate per side, cross-side network effects

### Industry-specific validation questions
- How do you solve the cold-start problem - which side do you seed first and how?
- What is your take rate, and how does it compare to benchmarks (Uber 25%, Airbnb 14%, eBay 13%)?
- What prevents buyers and sellers from going direct after the first transaction?
- What is the minimum viable liquidity - how many supply units per demand query before the marketplace "works"?

### Proposal/deck adjustments
- Section 2 (Industry): Address two-sided network effects explicitly
- Section 4 (Operations): Supply acquisition strategy, quality control, trust & safety
- Section 5 (Financial): Separate GMV from net revenue; show take rate evolution

## Hardware / IoT

### Key metrics
- BOM cost at prototype vs 1K vs 10K units, tooling amortization, MOQ
- Certification timeline and cost (UL, FCC, CE, FDA if medical)
- Supply chain lead times, single-source component risks
- Warranty/return rate, field failure rate

### Industry-specific validation questions
- What is your BOM at current volume vs target volume of 10K units?
- What certifications are required, and what is the timeline and cost?
- Who manufactures v1 - you, contract manufacturer, or partner? At what MOQ?
- What happens if your key component supplier has a 12-week delay?

### Proposal/deck adjustments
- Section 4 (Operations): Manufacturing plan, supplier relationships, certification timeline, quality testing
- Section 5 (Financial): Tooling costs as capex, BOM cost curve at scale, inventory carrying costs
- Section 6 (Timeline): Certification milestones as critical path items

## Biotech / Healthtech

### Key metrics
- Regulatory pathway (FDA 510(k) vs PMA vs De Novo; SaMD classification under EU MDR)
- Clinical trial design (phases, IRB approval, patient recruitment cost)
- Reimbursement pathway (CPT codes, payer willingness, HCPCS)
- Time-to-market including regulatory (often 2-7 years)

### Industry-specific validation questions
- What is your regulatory pathway - 510(k), PMA, or exempt? Estimated timeline and cost?
- Do you have IRB approval or a protocol for clinical validation?
- Who pays - patient, insurer, employer, hospital? Is there a reimbursement code?
- Are you classified as SaMD under FDA or EU MDR? What are the compliance implications?
- What is your clinical evidence strategy - retrospective data, prospective trial, or real-world evidence?

### Proposal/deck adjustments
- Section 4 (Operations): Regulatory timeline as critical path, clinical validation plan
- Section 6 (Timeline): Regulatory milestones explicitly mapped with go/no-go gates
- Section 7 (People): Clinical/regulatory expertise on team or advisory board

## CPG / D2C

### Key metrics
- Retail margin stack (manufacturer → distributor → retailer → consumer)
- Channel conflict (DTC vs wholesale pricing), MAP policies
- Shelf placement economics, slotting fees, velocity requirements
- Packaging cost per unit, shipping/fulfillment cost, return rate
- Repeat purchase rate, subscription vs one-time economics

### Industry-specific validation questions
- What is your retail margin stack from COGS to shelf price?
- Do you start DTC or wholesale? How do you avoid channel conflict?
- What is your packaging cost per unit and how does it affect margin?
- What is your customer repeat purchase rate, and how does it compare to category benchmarks?

### Proposal/deck adjustments
- Section 3 (Marketing): Channel strategy with margin impact per channel
- Section 5 (Financial): Include fulfillment, returns, and packaging in COGS
- Section 4 (Operations): Fulfillment infrastructure (3PL vs in-house), inventory management

---

## Fintech

### Key metrics
- Compliance requirements (KYC/AML, state money transmitter licenses, PCI-DSS)
- Banking partner or sponsor bank relationship status
- Regulatory capital requirements, trust account structures
- Transaction volume and fraud rate

### Industry-specific validation questions
- What licenses do you need - state money transmitter, broker-dealer, other?
- Do you have a banking partner or sponsor bank? What are their requirements and timeline?
- How do you handle KYC/AML compliance - build vs buy (e.g., Alloy, Plaid)?
- What happens if regulations change (e.g., CFPB rule changes, state-level restrictions)?

### Proposal/deck adjustments
- Section 4 (Operations): Compliance infrastructure, banking partnerships, fraud prevention
- Section 2 (Industry): Regulatory landscape as competitive moat or barrier
- Section 5 (Financial): Compliance costs as ongoing opex, not one-time

---

## Edtech

### Key metrics
- Institutional vs consumer sales cycle, procurement process (RFP, pilot, district-wide)
- Efficacy evidence (learning outcomes, completion rates, engagement metrics)
- FERPA/COPPA compliance if K-12 or university data
- Teacher/admin adoption rate vs student engagement

### Industry-specific validation questions
- Are you selling to institutions or consumers? What is the procurement cycle?
- What evidence do you have of learning outcome improvement?
- How do you handle student data privacy (FERPA, COPPA)?
- How do you get past the "pilot graveyard" - what converts a pilot to a paid contract?

### Proposal/deck adjustments
- Section 3 (Marketing): Institutional sales motion vs consumer growth
- Section 2 (Industry): Efficacy evidence as competitive differentiator
- Section 4 (Operations): Data privacy compliance infrastructure

---

## Climate / Energy

### Key metrics
- Carbon credit or offset economics, additionality proof
- Regulatory incentives (IRA credits, EU ETS, state rebates) and sunset risk
- Capital intensity and project finance structures
- Levelized cost comparisons to incumbent energy/process

### Industry-specific validation questions
- What regulatory incentives apply, and what happens if they sunset?
- What is the capital intensity per unit of output?
- How do you prove additionality for carbon claims?
- What is your levelized cost vs the incumbent solution, and at what scale do you reach parity?

### Proposal/deck adjustments
- Section 5 (Financial): Capital stack (equity, project finance, grants, tax credits)
- Section 2 (Industry): Policy risk as both opportunity and threat
- Section 6 (Timeline): Permitting and regulatory approval milestones
